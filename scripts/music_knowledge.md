## Techniques Learned
- counterpoint: adding a second melodic voice in contrary motion
- arpeggios: breaking chords into sequential note patterns
- ostinato: repeating rhythmic/melodic figures for grounding
- pedal_tone: sustained bass note under changing harmony
- dynamics_swell: volume crescendo/decrescendo waves
- modal_mixture: borrowing notes from parallel modes (not yet implemented)
- suspensions: delayed chord resolutions creating tension (not yet implemented)
- call_response: melodic dialogue between instruments (not yet implemented)
- polyrhythm: layering different time divisions (not yet implemented)

## Scales & Modes Known
- major pentatonic (contemplative, serene)
- natural minor (melancholic)
- lydian (ethereal) — raised 4th gives dreamy quality
- phrygian (nocturnal) — flat 2 gives dark/spanish feel
- ionian/major (hopeful, triumphant)
- double harmonic (mysterious) — augmented 2nd intervals
- diminished/octatonic (anxious) — symmetrical, unstable
- hungarian minor (cosmic) — exotic, dramatic
- dorian (bittersweet) — minor with bright 6th
- whole tone (dreamlike) — no resolution, floating

## Composition Insights
- Phrase structure matters: 4-8 note groups with rests between sound more musical
- Ghost notes (very low velocity) add depth and texture
- Delayed melody entry creates anticipation
- Contrary motion between voices creates independence
- Ostinato figures anchor a piece, let melody float above
- Tempo affects emotional perception more than most parameters
